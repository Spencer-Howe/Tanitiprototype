FAQ

The best time to visit Taniti is from April to October when the weather is most favorable.

Visa requirements depend on your country of origin. Please check with the Taniti immigration office for the most current information.

Taniti offers various transportation options including public buses, taxis, rental cars, and bike rentals. Public buses serve Taniti City and private buses serve the rest of the island.

Taniti is generally very safe for tourists. Violent crime is rare, but visitors should be aware of pickpocketing and petty theft in tourist areas.

Taniti uses the U.S. dollar as its currency, but many businesses also accept euros and yen. Major credit cards are widely accepted.

The official language of Taniti is Tanitian, but many younger residents speak fluent English. In rural areas, especially among older residents, very little English is spoken.

Taniti offers a wide range of accommodation options, from budget-friendly hostels to luxury resorts. There are also family-owned hotels and bed and breakfasts.

Yes, Taniti has a hospital and several clinics. The hospital is staffed with multilingual employees and offers comprehensive medical services.

Tourists can enjoy a variety of activities in Taniti including beach visits, rainforest hikes, volcano tours, snorkeling, zip-lining, visiting museums and art galleries, and much more.
