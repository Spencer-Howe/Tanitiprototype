Discover the Hidden Gem of Taniti

Explore the untouched beauty and vibrant culture of Taniti, a small tropical paradise in the Pacific.

About the Island

Taniti is a small, tropical island in the Pacific. With an area of less than 500 square miles, it features varied terrain, including sandy and rocky beaches, a small harbor, lush rainforests, and a mountainous interior with a small active volcano. The island's indigenous population is about 20,000. Traditionally reliant on fishing and agriculture, the Tanitian economy has recently seen a rise in tourism.

Tourist Needs

Taniti offers a variety of services to meet the needs of tourists, including:

  • Restaurants: 10 restaurants with diverse cuisine options.
  • Grocery Stores: 2 supermarkets, 2 smaller grocery stores, and a 24-hour convenience store.
  • Lodging: A range of accommodations from hostels to a four-star resort.
  • Entertainment: Activities like beach visits, rainforest exploration, and visits to the volcano.
  • Transportation: Air and cruise ship arrivals, with various ground transportation options.

Sightseeing

Popular activities include:

  • Exploring Taniti City and its architecture.
  • Boat or bus tours of the island.
  • Rainforest hikes.
  • Visiting the active volcano.

Frequently Requested Information

  • Power outlets: 120 volts (same as the United States).
  • Alcohol restrictions: Not served/sold between midnight and 9:00 a.m.
  • Drinking age: 18 (not strictly enforced).
  • Languages: English is commonly spoken by younger Tanitians.
  • Healthcare: One hospital and several clinics, many multilingual employees.
  • Crime: Violent crime is rare; petty crime is increasing.
  • Holidays: Many attractions/restaurants close on holidays; plan accordingly.
  • Currency: U.S. dollar; euros and yen are also accepted. Major credit cards accepted.
